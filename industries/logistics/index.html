Where logistics teams start

Begin with the asset categories that cause the highest downstream disruption when missing - typically high-value RTIs, pallet jacks, and shared dock equipment. Set geofences for key transfer points and assign alert ownership.

Once the workflow is proven in one hub or corridor, scale to more facilities and asset types using the same naming and alert standards.

Operational result

Movement timelines replace manual reconstruction during loss investigations and chain-of-custody reviews. Warehouse workers spend less time searching for shared equipment at shift start. Dwell-time patterns surface so operations can address bottlenecks before they cause detention charges.

For non-powered assets that move unpredictably between facilities, TagLogger delivers the accountability layer that barcode scanning misses and GPS tracking can't justify at scale.

Fills the gap between barcode scans

Barcode and RFID only track assets at fixed scan points. TagLogger fills the visibility gap for assets in transit, in yards, or at customer sites between scans.

Practical for non-powered assets at scale
