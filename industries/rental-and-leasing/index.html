What rental operations are up against

Rental inventory constantly moves between customers, yards, and branches. Between physical counts, 5–15% of the fleet may be unaccounted for — not on rent, not in the yard, status unknown. Traditional GPS costs $15–30 per asset per month.

1

5–15% Ghost Fleet

ARA benchmarks target 72% on-rent / 20% yard-ready / 8% non-rental-ready ([Rental Equipment Register](https://www.rermag.com/features/article/20953419/the-numbers-behind-the-numbers)). In practice, 5–15% lands in a status-unknown bucket between physical counts.

2

15–25% Overdue Returns

A significant share of rental contracts go past their return date. Without location data, recovery teams start from zero with phone calls.

3

GPS Costs Don't Scale

Traditional GPS tracking runs $15–30 per asset per month in cellular fees alone. For a 200-unit fleet, that's $36,000–$72,000 a year.

4

60–70% Time Utilization

Average time utilization sits around 60–70%. Every idle day at the wrong branch is lost revenue that visibility could prevent.

The ghost fleet problem

Rental inventory constantly moves between customers, yards, and branches. Manual yard audits consume hours of labor and the data goes stale within days. Between counts, equipment can be overdue, misplaced, or sitting idle at the wrong branch - and nobody knows until someone checks.

TagLogger replaces point-in-time physical counts with continuous visibility. Branch managers and dispatch teams can see where assets were last active, so recovery and rebalancing decisions happen faster and with better data.

How rental teams start

Begin with the asset categories that drive the most shrink and margin risk - typically generators, compactors, light towers, and trailers. Set geofences for yards and depots, assign alert ownership, and build return-monitoring workflows.

As the process proves out, expand to additional inventory classes and branches using the same naming and alert standards.

Why this improves time utilization

Average time utilization across general rental fleets sits around 60-70%. Every idle day at the wrong location is lost revenue. When branch and ops teams share real-time movement context, they can reposition equipment before utilization drops.

Last-seen data also reduces the labor spent chasing overdue returns - recovery teams have a starting point instead of starting from zero.
