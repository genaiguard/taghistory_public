Where container operations break down

Recycling and donation containers placed across retail lots and host sites can move without warning. When that happens, operators need to know where the container went and when it left its assigned location.

Without reliable tracking, teams end up relying on phone calls, site checks, and memory to piece events together. That slows recovery and makes movement disputes harder to resolve.

Why teams choose TagLogger over traditional GPS trackers

Traditional GPS tracking usually means higher device cost, ongoing cellular connectivity fees, and a separate software subscription. For large fleets of non-powered containers, that stack can be expensive to deploy and maintain.

TagLogger is built for lower-overhead container tracking. Teams can use compact hardware, avoid separate SIM management, and choose battery options that better fit distributed outdoor deployments.

Rollout model for container security

Start with high-risk container clusters near retail corridors, assign alert ownership, and set geofence standards by site type. Once workflows are proven, expand region by region with the same operating playbook.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do geofence alerts help when a container moves?

You define approved zones around host sites and service areas. When a tagged container crosses a boundary, TagLogger can surface an alert so dispatch and field teams can respond while recovery is still realistic.

Do we need cellular plans or wired GPS on every container?

No. TagLogger is built around Find My–compatible tags, so you avoid per-container SIM fees and dedicated GPS wiring. Coverage depends on the Find My network where tags are located.

What battery life can we expect in the field?

Battery life depends on the tag hardware you choose. Teams often use compact tags with up to about one year of battery, or extended-life options suited to long deployments outdoors. Replace or service tags on a schedule that matches your rollout.

Can we share movement proof with customers, landlords, or insurers?

Yes. Secure share links let you provide external stakeholders with the context they need without granting full account access—useful during service disputes or incident follow-up.
