Waste and recycling visibility gaps

Mobile containers and route assets move constantly between yards, transfer stations, and customer sites. Without shared visibility, recovery and service verification become slow and expensive.

TagLogger gives operations and dispatch one source of movement truth so teams can confirm where assets were and respond to exceptions faster.

What operations teams track first

  • Roll-off and specialty containers with high theft or misplacement risk
  • Route support assets that frequently move between districts or depots
  • Containers tied to recurring customer-site disputes or missed-service investigations

How TagLogger supports waste and recycling workflows

  • Location history and last-seen context for recovery and investigation workflows
  • Geofence alerts around yards, transfer points, and customer zones to catch unauthorized movement
  • Multi-user access so dispatch, customer service, and field leadership can work from the same data

Rollout approach

Start with high-loss categories and one operating district, then expand route by route after alert ownership and escalation are proven.

Use standardized naming by service area, asset class, and ownership so your tracking data stays usable as deployment scales.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we track recycling and waste containers at customer sites?

Yes. TagLogger is commonly used for container-level visibility across customer properties, transfer locations, and yards. You can review last-seen location and movement history when containers are reported missing or moved unexpectedly.

Can this help with container theft or unauthorized pickup?

Yes. Geofence alerts can flag movement outside approved zones, and timeline history helps teams investigate incidents quickly. That gives operations a faster path to recovery and a clearer audit trail for follow-up.

Can we monitor route trucks too?

TagLogger can support tracking for route support vehicles and mobile fleet-adjacent assets where AirTag workflows are appropriate. Many operators pair this with existing telematics for primary vehicle tracking and use TagLogger for shared equipment and container workflows.

How does this help with service disputes and missed pickups?

Movement history gives customer service and operations a shared timeline to validate where assets were and when. That reduces back-and-forth during missed-service investigations and helps teams resolve disputes with evidence.

What should we tag first?

Start with assets that generate the most recovery work or service disruption when lost: roll-off containers, specialty recycling bins, and high-mobility route equipment. Then expand to broader container inventory once alerts and response workflows are established.
