Mixed-asset fleet tracking challenges

Fleet performance often depends on more than vehicle location. Trailers, containers, and shared route equipment can block service windows when they are missing or misplaced.

TagLogger gives dispatch and operations one timeline of movement for those fleet-adjacent assets, improving exception handling and reducing coordination lag.

Fleet workflows supported by TagLogger

  • Track fleet-adjacent assets across depots, routes, and customer zones
  • Use geofence alerts to detect unauthorized movement or late returns
  • Share movement context across dispatch, operations, and field supervisors

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this replace vehicle telematics?

No. TagLogger is typically used for fleet-adjacent asset visibility such as trailers, containers, and shared equipment. Many teams use TagLogger alongside telematics to cover the non-vehicle assets that still impact route performance.

Can dispatch teams monitor multiple asset classes?

Yes. TagLogger provides one interface for equipment and other tagged assets across your operation. You can view everything together or filter by asset type, location, or team.

Can we notify teams about boundary violations?

Yes. Geofence entry and exit alerts help surface movement exceptions quickly. Configure zones around depots, customer sites, or approved areas so your team is notified when tagged assets cross those boundaries.

Is location history available for service reviews?

Yes. Historical movement data supports operational reviews and follow-up investigations. Timeline and map history help resolve disputes and improve planning for future assignments.

How do we get started with mixed-asset tracking?

Identify categories that cause the most delay when unavailable, tag those assets first, and set geofences for key locations. Once response workflows are stable, expand to additional routes and asset classes.
