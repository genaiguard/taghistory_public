Why teams use TagLogger for fleet management

Many fleet tracking setups rely on wired GPS devices, recurring communication charges, and hardware that needs regular power or battery maintenance. That can make rollout slower and more expensive than it needs to be.

TagLogger gives teams a simpler way to track vehicles by using AirTags and long-life battery options that can be placed in the vehicle without wiring. You get visibility, movement history, and event notifications without adding separate monthly cellular or satellite communication fees.

Fleet workflows supported by TagLogger

  • Track vehicles across depots, routes, customer locations, and operating zones
  • View fleet activity on a single dashboard shared across dispatch, operations, and field teams
  • Use geofence alerts to detect unauthorized movement or late returns
  • Keep a documented log of vehicle movements and events for follow-up, audits, and operational reviews

Operational result

Fleet teams get visibility on a single dashboard, so dispatch, operations, and field staff can respond faster when something changes.

Movement history and event records create clearer documentation, quicker response times, and an audit log for follow-up.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can dispatch teams monitor multiple asset classes?

Yes. TagLogger provides one interface for vehicles and other tagged assets across your operation. You can view everything together or filter by asset type, location, or team.

Can we notify teams about boundary violations?

Yes. Geofence entry and exit alerts help surface movement exceptions quickly. Configure zones around depots, customer sites, or approved areas so your team is notified when tagged assets cross those boundaries.
